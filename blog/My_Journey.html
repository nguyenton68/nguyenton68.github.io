My Journey to be a software engineer

Posted by Nguyen Ton on January 18th, 2021

When I was in senior year in high school, it was hard for me to pick a major. At that time, banking was hot, so I was not an exception, I wanted to apply for banking school, but I was lack of confidence and also I concerned about expensive tuition that would be a burden for my parent, so finally, I chose college of education (free tuition). After enrolled in chemistry for 1 month, I switched to physics.

After finished undergrad, I continue to do Phd in Physics. After 5 years in PhD, I came to a point that I needed to change major, because my husband moved to financial industry, so solution for us to be together was I would become a software engineer. One other reason is there was one time that I worked in the lab and I almost got my eye burned by strong power laser. I was scared to death after that accident. In addition, Software engineer is a very hot industry, many jobs available, literally everywhere US, Europe, Asia.

I don’t know since when I started to idolize Steve Jobs and Elon Musk. It gave me motivation to switch. I remembered when I submitted my software engineer application to Tesla, I was so happy and dreaming one day I would met Elon in person.

This career jump was quite not easy. Mostly because my major is physics. But I spent time self-study about basic of computer science. Gosh, it was very frustrated at first, I did not have any foundation in programming. In Phd, I wrote code, but just write code so it can run and give output, and don’t care about time complexity and don’t care about data structure. The bible book that I used: cracking the coding interview. It was very helpful. Everything was so new for me from the first round of reading, but I read again and again. I practice a lot on leetcode.com website, where I used to spent most of my time to do those algorithm problems. Till now, I’m still very lame in programming.

My journey can’t go anywhere without help from friends. They gave me suggestions, instructions, one of them even spent time to go through some algorithm problems with me.

Companies that I had online acessment: pure storage, okta, MasterCard, visa, etc. Companies that I had onsite: walmart, google, JP morgan. Companies that filter my cv based on my physics background and my GPA: amazon, bloomberg, oracle, and several hundred others.

So I started with an internship where I worked on cryptography, it was quite a very exciting field, but unfortunately I did not get return offer.

Google onsite was an unforgettable experience for me. My first time visit Google campus in mountain view. My eyes were wide-opened and filled with excitements. My first interview was a cute Japanese guy (I’m sure I’m older than him), he has terrible accent. Unfortunately, lucky angle was on vacation, so I will apply again.

After so many trials, finally, I got offer from JP Morgan. Till now, sometimes, I still don’t believe that I’m a software engineer.
