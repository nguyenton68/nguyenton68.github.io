Some basic introduction in Medical Image search

  1. Introduction to Magnetic Resonance Image (MRI):
    • Video for MRI (here)
    • Principle of Nuclear Magnetic Resonance (NMR): flip of the spin (He3 in my thesis) in a strong constant magnetic field by the perturbation of a weak RF field. The flip induced signal in pair of pickup coil. This signal is proportional to the spin polarization.
    • Principle of Pulsed NMR: this polarimetry is somewhat similar to NMR, but instead of perturb the spin with a gradually sweep of the RF frequency, we send an RF signal right at the resonance frequency (Lamor Frequency), the RF is sent for a very short time, after that, RF is disconnected. The signal is measured right after the RF is turned off.
  2. Positron Emission Tomography: is an imaging technique use to visualize and measure metabolic processes in our body.
    • Principle: localization of a tumor is determined by injecting a tracer (usually FDG because natural analog to glucose and is trapped in tissue until radioactive decay). The tracer will emit a positron who will travel a short distance within the patient. Finally it will annihilate into two photons. The location of tumor can be determined by detection of 2 annihilation photons along a line between 2 detectors.
    • PET is used for imaging for diagnosis, staging, recurrence detection, and therapeutic monitoring.
    • The key information for a good quality PET image is a combination of Position, Energy and Timing information. Several factors determine image quality are: spatial resolution, detector resolution, depth-of-interaction, Signal-to-Noise-Ratio (Sensitivity, energy resolution).
